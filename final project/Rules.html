Learn Pickleball

Rules of the Game

Pickleball is played either as singles or doubles on a court similar to a badminton court, using a paddle and a plastic ball with holes. The game begins with an underhand serve made diagonally across the court. Players must allow the ball to bounce once on each side before volleys are allowed, which is known as the “double bounce rule.” Points can only be scored by the serving team, and games are typically played to 11 points, with a team needing to win by at least 2 points. Players are not allowed to volley the ball while standing in the non-volley zone, also known as “the kitchen,” which is the area close to the net.

Pro Tips

  • 🎾 Keep your paddle up and ready at all times to react quickly
  • 🎾 Aim your shots toward your opponent’s feet to make returns harder
  • 🎾 Use soft shots like dinks to control the pace of the game
  • 🎾 Communicate with your partner when playing doubles
  • 🎾 Stay out of the kitchen unless the ball bounces first
  • 🎾 Focus on placement and control instead of power
